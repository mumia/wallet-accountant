Nygard-minimal template (Michael Nygard, 2011 — https://cognitect.com/blog/2011/11/15/documenting-architecture-decisions).

This is the classic minimal ADR format: five sections, no frontmatter
beyond status, "short enough to read in 5 minutes". Nygard's original
intent was that ADRs should be cheap to write and cheap to read, so
teams write them often instead of treating them as ceremony.

edikt ships this as a reference template alongside adr-madr-extended.md.
Your project picks one (or writes its own) during /edikt:init.

Choose Nygard-minimal when:
- You want ADRs to be written frequently and kept short
- Your team values brevity over exhaustive alternative analysis
- Most decisions don't warrant a 30-minute write-up

If you prefer a richer format with Decision Drivers, Considered Options,
Pros and Cons, and Confirmation, see adr-madr-extended.md.
